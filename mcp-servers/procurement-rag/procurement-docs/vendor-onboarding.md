# Vendor Onboarding Process

## Overview
All new vendors must complete the RTL vendor onboarding process before any work is dispatched or any purchase order is issued. This ensures compliance with Ontario regulations (WSIB, MTO, TDG), protects RTL against uninsured loss, and keeps the approved vendor list current.

## Vendor Categories
RTL onboards vendors in the following categories. Each has slightly different documentation requirements (see Section 3 and Section 5).

### Reefer Service Vendors
Authorized Carrier Transicold or Thermo King service centers, mobile reefer technicians, refrigerant handlers.

### Trailer OEM and Parts Vendors
Trailer dealers (Utility, Great Dane, Wabash National), parts wholesalers, refrigerant suppliers, electrical and brake component suppliers.

### Tire and Roadside Vendors
Commercial tire suppliers, retread services, 24/7 roadside assistance, heavy-duty towing.

### Operational Service Vendors
Fleet wash and sanitation, fleet telematics, fleet insurance brokers, DOT/MTO compliance auditors.

## Step 1: Initial Vendor Assessment
Before engaging a new vendor, the requesting department must provide:
- Vendor name and primary contact
- Vendor category (from Section 2 above)
- Description of services or goods needed
- Estimated annual or one-time contract value (CAD)
- Business justification
- Urgency or service-by date

## Step 2: Vendor Risk Assessment
The procurement team conducts a risk assessment based on:
- Annual contract value
- Type of work (mechanical service, parts supply, on-road service, data/telematics)
- Whether the vendor will operate on RTL premises or interact with RTL trailers on the road
- Whether the vendor handles regulated materials (refrigerant, hazmat)
- Industry reputation and reference checks

Risk levels:
- **Low Risk**: under $25,000 CAD per year, no on-premises work, established Ontario vendor with verifiable references
- **Medium Risk**: $25,000 to $100,000 CAD per year, some on-premises or on-road work, or new vendor
- **High Risk**: over $100,000 CAD per year, significant on-premises access, refrigerant handling, or cross-border operations

## Step 3: Required Documentation
All vendors must submit:

### Basic Requirements (All Vendors)
- Business registration (Ontario Corporation Profile or Master Business Licence)
- HST registration number (if the vendor is required to collect HST)
- Banking information for EFT payments (void cheque or pre-authorized debit form)
- Certificate of General Liability Insurance, minimum 2 million CAD per occurrence
- WSIB clearance certificate (Ontario Workplace Safety and Insurance Board), current and showing no outstanding premiums

### Additional Requirements for Service Vendors
- Commercial Auto / Garage Liability Insurance, minimum 2 million CAD, naming RTL as an additional insured if work is performed on RTL premises
- Proof of authorized service center status (for Carrier Transicold or Thermo King work)
- EPA Section 608 certification or Environment Canada Refrigerant Handler certification for any vendor handling refrigerants
- Transportation of Dangerous Goods (TDG) certification for vendors transporting refrigerant
- Workplace Hazardous Materials Information System (WHMIS) compliance attestation for shop personnel

### Additional Requirements for Tire and Roadside Vendors
- Heavy-duty wrecker authority (where applicable)
- Cross-border roadside coverage map (for vendors used on US routes)
- Commercial Auto Insurance, minimum 5 million CAD for towing operations

### Additional Requirements for Cross-Border Vendors
- IRS Form W-9 (for US-based vendors)
- US DOT number (for US trucking-adjacent vendors)
- Documented USD invoicing terms and remittance address

## Step 4: Reference Checks
For Medium and High Risk vendors:
- Minimum 2 reference letters from comparable Canadian fleet operators
- Site visit to the vendor's facility (for shop-based service vendors)
- Verification of authorized service center status directly with the OEM

## Step 5: Legal Review and Approval Thresholds

### Contracts under 25,000 CAD per year
- Standard service agreement or purchase order template applies
- Fleet Manager approval
- Typical turnaround: 2 to 3 business days

### Contracts 25,000 to 100,000 CAD per year
- Legal review of liability, indemnification, and warranty clauses
- Controller approval
- Typical turnaround: 5 to 7 business days

### Contracts over 100,000 CAD per year
- Full legal review of all terms
- CFO approval required
- Board notification for contracts over 250,000 CAD
- Typical turnaround: 10 to 15 business days

## Step 6: Add to Approved Vendor List
Once all approvals are complete:
1. Vendor is added to the RTL approved vendor database
2. Vendor receives a welcome packet with:
   - Payment terms and EFT remittance details
   - Invoice formatting requirements (must reference RTL PO number and unit number for trailer work)
   - Yard access procedures (for on-premises vendors)
   - Escalation contacts for emergencies and disputes
3. PO authority is established in the accounting system

## Annual Recertification
Approved vendors must recertify annually by submitting:
- Updated Certificate of Insurance
- Current WSIB clearance certificate
- Refreshed Carrier or Thermo King authorization (for OEM service vendors)
- Confirmation of TDG, EPA 608, or WHMIS certifications, where applicable

Vendors that fail to recertify within 30 days of expiry are placed on hold (no new POs or service dispatches) until documentation is current.

## Disqualification
A vendor may be removed from the approved list for:
- Lapsed WSIB or insurance coverage
- Loss of OEM authorization (Carrier or Thermo King)
- Workmanship failures affecting safety or DOT/MTO compliance
- Repeated invoice or pricing disputes
- Conflict of interest or breach of vendor code of conduct
