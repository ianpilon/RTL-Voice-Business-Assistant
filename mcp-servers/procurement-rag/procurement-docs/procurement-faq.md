# Procurement Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## Submitting Procurement Requests

### Q: How do I submit a procurement request?
Call the RTL Procurement Office or submit a request through the procurement portal. You'll need to provide:
- Budget code or charge number
- Description of what's needed
- Estimated cost (CAD)
- Required milestones or service-by date
- Trailer unit number (if the request is trailer-specific)

The voice AI can validate your request for completeness and flag missing fields before it reaches the procurement team.

### Q: What information do I need before calling?
Have your budget code, the trailer unit number (if applicable), and your cost estimate ready. If you're requesting a new vendor relationship, also have the vendor's name and category (reefer service, parts, tire, etc.).

### Q: What's the typical turnaround time?
- Under 25,000 CAD: 2 to 3 business days for approval
- 25,000 to 100,000 CAD: 5 to 7 business days
- Over 100,000 CAD: 10 to 15 business days for full approval cycle

### Q: My request is urgent — what do I do?
For roadside reefer failures and load-at-risk situations, dispatch can authorize emergency service up to 10,000 CAD without prior procurement approval (see Emergency Authority below). For non-emergency urgent requests, contact the Fleet Manager directly to expedite.

## Vendor Onboarding

### Q: How long does vendor onboarding take?
- Low-risk Ontario vendors (under 25,000 CAD per year): 5 to 7 business days
- Medium-risk vendors (25,000 to 100,000 CAD per year): 2 to 3 weeks
- High-risk vendors (over 100,000 CAD per year, or cross-border): 3 to 6 weeks

### Q: What documents does a new vendor need to provide?
At minimum: Ontario business registration, HST registration number, banking info for EFT, Certificate of General Liability Insurance (2 million CAD minimum), and a current WSIB clearance certificate. Service vendors also need commercial auto insurance. See the vendor onboarding policy for the full list by vendor category.

### Q: Can I use a vendor who isn't on the approved list?
No, with one exception: emergency roadside dispatches when no approved vendor is reasonably available. In that case, the dispatch is authorized as a one-off; the vendor is added to a watch list and must complete onboarding within 30 days to receive further work.

### Q: How often do approved vendors need to recertify?
Annually. Vendors submit updated insurance certificates, current WSIB clearance, and any expired OEM authorizations (Carrier, Thermo King). Vendors that fail to recertify within 30 days of expiry are placed on hold.

## Insurance and Compliance

### Q: What insurance does a vendor need?
- General Liability: minimum 2 million CAD per occurrence
- Commercial Auto / Garage Liability: minimum 2 million CAD for service vendors performing work on RTL trailers
- Heavy-duty towing vendors: minimum 5 million CAD commercial auto
- WSIB clearance certificate, current and showing no outstanding premiums

### Q: What's WSIB and why does it matter?
WSIB is the Ontario Workplace Safety and Insurance Board, which provides workers' compensation insurance. Most vendors who employ people in Ontario must be registered with WSIB. A WSIB clearance certificate confirms they're in good standing and protects RTL from being held liable for a vendor's unfunded workers' comp claims.

### Q: What about CVOR?
CVOR is the Commercial Vehicle Operator's Registration issued by the Ontario Ministry of Transportation. Vendors operating commercial trucks on Ontario roads (towing, mobile reefer service) should have an active CVOR. Ask for the CVOR number during onboarding.

### Q: What's TDG and when does it apply?
TDG is the federal Transportation of Dangerous Goods regulation. It applies to vendors transporting refrigerant in quantities above the regulatory threshold. Refrigerant suppliers and roadside service vendors carrying refrigerant must have TDG-certified personnel and proper labeling.

## Refrigerant Handling

### Q: Who can handle refrigerant on our trailers?
Only technicians certified under Environment Canada's refrigerant management regulations (or, for cross-border work, the US EPA Section 608 program). All RTL reefer service vendors are required to maintain current certification as a condition of approved status.

### Q: What refrigerants do our trailers use?
The fleet runs primarily on R-452A (current generation) with some older units on R-404A. Both require certified handling. R-404A is being phased out under HFC management regulations; new trailer specs default to R-452A or other low-GWP alternatives.

### Q: Who supplies our refrigerant?
Cardinal Refrigerant Supply is our approved bulk supplier. They handle R-452A and R-404A in 50-pound drums and provide EPA Section 608 certified handling. Bulk-only — they don't supply small quantities for one-off jobs.

### Q: What if a vendor needs to top up refrigerant on the road?
Mobile reefer service vendors (Thermal Edge Mobile Service) carry small refrigerant quantities for roadside top-ups under their TDG authorization. Larger refrigerant work (recovery and recharge) goes to a service center.

## Repair Authorization

### Q: How much can a shop tech authorize without procurement approval?
Shop supervisors can authorize repairs up to 5,000 CAD per trailer per visit. Repairs above that require Fleet Manager approval. PM (preventive maintenance) work under an existing service agreement is pre-authorized and doesn't need a new approval.

### Q: What about emergency authority on the road?
When a reefer unit fails on the road and the load is at risk, the dispatcher may authorize roadside repair up to 10,000 CAD without prior approval. The Fleet Manager must be notified by the next business day. Documentation (vendor invoice, vendor name, trailer unit number, load impact) must follow within 5 days.

### Q: What if the repair quote exceeds the trailer's residual value?
For older trailers (typically over 10 years), repair quotes that exceed 50 percent of the trailer's current market value trigger a "repair vs replace" review with the Controller. RTL-1008 and RTL-1013 are both candidates for this review.

## Cross-Border Work

### Q: Can a US-based vendor work on our trailers in Canada?
Yes, but they must complete vendor onboarding including providing a W-9 (in lieu of HST registration), proof of US business registration, and proof that they can legally perform the work in Canada (typically a B-1 business visitor entry or work permit, depending on duration and nature of the work).

### Q: Can a Canadian vendor work on our trailers in the US?
Yes for roadside dispatches; check the vendor's commercial auto policy covers US operations and that drivers carry proper customs documentation (B-13A or similar). Longer engagements may require the vendor to register with the US DOT.

### Q: How do we pay a US vendor?
US-based vendors invoice in USD and are paid by wire transfer. The accounting system records both the USD invoice and the CAD equivalent at the Bank of Canada noon rate on the invoice date.

## Lease vs Buy

### Q: When does RTL lease a trailer to a customer vs sell?
RTL is in the leasing and repair business — selling trailers is rare. Customers (Maple Logistics, Northern Foods Co., Apex Distribution, ColdHaul Inc.) lease trailers from RTL on 12 to 36 month terms. Sales of used units are typically only when a customer asks to buy out an existing lease or when a trailer is being retired and a buyer steps forward.

### Q: How is the lease rate set?
Lease rates are based on unit age, condition, reefer specifications, and the lease term. Newer units (less than 3 years) command a premium; older units (over 8 years) are leased at reduced rates that reflect higher expected maintenance.

### Q: What happens at the end of a customer lease?
The trailer returns to RTL's yard for an inspection. The customer is responsible for returning the unit in clean, food-grade condition. Damage beyond normal wear is billed back to the customer per the lease agreement.

## Approval Tiers

### Q: Who approves what?
- Shop Supervisor: under 5,000 CAD parts and service
- Dispatcher: emergency roadside up to 10,000 CAD
- Fleet Manager: 5,000 to 25,000 CAD operating spend
- Controller: 25,000 to 100,000 CAD, plus MSAs and currency exposure decisions
- CFO: 100,000 to 500,000 CAD, plus all capital purchases regardless of amount
- CEO with board notification: over 500,000 CAD

### Q: What's the approval limit for emergency reefer repairs?
Up to 10,000 CAD can be authorized by dispatch without prior approval if the load is at risk. Above that, contact the Fleet Manager. For roadside repairs over 25,000 CAD, the Controller must be reached even if it takes a phone call after hours.

## Disputes and Escalation

### Q: A vendor charged me more than expected. What do I do?
Flag the invoice for dispute within 10 days of receipt. The disputed line items are withheld from payment; undisputed lines are paid on standard terms. Procurement contacts the vendor to resolve. Target resolution is 15 business days.

### Q: A repair didn't actually fix the problem. What do I do?
Document the issue (photos, trailer downtime, follow-up cost) and contact the vendor under the workmanship warranty in the service agreement (minimum 90 days on labour). If the vendor disputes the claim, escalate to the Controller.

### Q: How do I escalate beyond a vendor?
Standard escalation path: Fleet Manager, then Controller, then CFO. Insurance-related disputes can also be escalated to Hayward Fleet Insurance Brokers for broker-handled resolution.

## Emergency Procedures

### Q: A reefer unit failed on the road and the load is at risk. What do I do?
1. Dispatch contacts Thermal Edge Mobile Service or SureRoad Roadside Network depending on what's needed
2. Up to 10,000 CAD is pre-authorized
3. Document the trailer unit number, driver, load, and incident details
4. Notify the Fleet Manager by the next business day
5. Submit follow-up paperwork (vendor invoice, photos if any) within 5 days

### Q: A customer's leased trailer broke down. Who's responsible?
RTL retains responsibility for reefer unit maintenance under the lease. Tires, brakes, and incidental damage are the customer's responsibility. For a reefer failure, dispatch authorizes roadside service the same way as for an RTL-operated unit and recovers cost from the customer's lease account where applicable.

### Q: A trailer is involved in an accident. What's the procedure?
1. Driver safety first; ensure emergency services are called if needed
2. Contact SureRoad Roadside Network for recovery and tow
3. Notify Hayward Fleet Insurance Brokers within 24 hours
4. Preserve documentation (police report, photos, witness info)
5. Hold the trailer at a designated facility for insurance inspection before any repair work begins
